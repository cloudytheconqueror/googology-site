Introduction

This is my take on creating and maintaining a long list about large numbers. After reading a bunch of lists by others (see below), I decided to create my own list that hopefully fits my ideals and includes major googolisms, numbers with interesting properties, and numbers I have interest in.

The list is comprised of 8 parts (this may change) for the finite numbers, plus one for infinite numbers and one for other stuff.

Like Sbiis Saibian's and Cookiefonster's lists, each part is divided into ranges and each range is color-coded.

This list includes significant numbers (with descriptions), as well as a bunch of less significant numbers to show how far the milestone numbers are from each other.

Each entry includes the name of the number (if there's one), the expression/approximation of the number, and likely also include a description if it is not a minor entry. The names and definitions of significant numbers are shown in larger font for easier identification.


The division of this list is not finalized and may be changed over time.

PART 1: Non-positive numbers, 0 ~ 1000

PART 2: 1000 to 10^10^6

PART 3: 10^10^6 to 10^^^10^100

PART 4: Up to order type w^3

PART 5: Up to order type epsilon-zero

PART 6: Up to order type gamma-zero

PART 7: Up to order type BHO

PART 8: Beyond order type BHO

Large number lists by others

Of course, I'm not the first person who came up with the idea of creating a general-purpose large number list. There are other great lists out there:

Cookie Fonster's Pointless Gigantic List of Numbers (PGLN), the list that inspired the creation of this list

Sbiis Saibian's Ultimate Large Numbers List (ULNL), a number list focused on numbers notable to googology, albeit a bit outdated on larger entries

Robert Munafo's Notable Properties of Specific Numbers, one of the oldest list of notable numbers
